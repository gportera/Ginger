Travel in Italy

Plan your trip!

Italy offers a wide range of travel experiences, from historic cities to coastal villages and mountain landscapes. Visitors often start in major cities such as Rome, Florence, or Venice. Rome is known for landmarks like the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and the Vatican. Florence is a center of Renaissance art and architecture, with museums containing works by Michelangelo and Botticelli. Venice is famous for its canals, historic bridges, and island districts.

Cities

Outside the major cities, travelers can explore regions like Tuscany, known for rolling hills, vineyards, and small towns such as Siena and San Gimignano. The Amalfi Coast offers dramatic seaside cliffs and towns such as Positano and Amalfi, which are popular for their scenic views and Mediterranean atmosphere. Northern Italy includes the Italian Lakes, such as Lake Como and Lake Garda, which are surrounded by mountains and popular for boating and hiking.

Beyond the City

Food

Italian food varies by region. Travelers can expect pasta dishes in many areas, but specialties differ: Rome has carbonara and cacio e pepe, Naples is known for pizza, and Emilia-Romagna is known for dishes such as lasagna and tortellini. Gelato is widely available in most cities and towns.

Climate
